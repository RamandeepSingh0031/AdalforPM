## The Problem

### For Pet Owners:
- **Cost barrier:** In-clinic vet visits cost $70-174 (dogs), $53-124 (cats). 52% of pet owners skip needed vet care due to cost.
- **Access gap:** Appointments booked weeks out. No care available after hours or weekends.
- **Anxiety:** "Is this serious or can I wait?" → Uncertainty drives expensive emergency visits.
- **Geographic inequality:** Rural areas have few vet clinics (70.4% of practices don't offer telehealth).

### For Veterinarians:
- **Burnout:** High-volume practices stretched thin. Can't see all patients who need care.
- **Capacity ceiling:** Revenue limited by clinic hours (typically 9-5, Mon-Fri).
- **Administrative drag:** Paper charts, phone scheduling, manual follow-ups.
- **Untapped revenue:** 30% of practices offer telehealth; 70% don't (huge growth opportunity).

---

## The Solution

### For Pet Owners:
- **Instant access:** Book a virtual vet appointment within minutes, not weeks
- **Affordable:** $35-50 per consultation vs. $70-174 in-clinic
- **Convenience:** Video from home, 24/7 availability, prescription delivery
- **Continuity:** Digital medical records follow pets across platforms
- **AI triage (coming in beta):** Smart symptom checker will recommend urgency level before the appointment — reducing anxiety and helping owners decide whether to book now or wait

### For Veterinarians:
- **Revenue expansion:** Telehealth beyond clinic hours (asynchronous + sync)
- **Efficiency:** Automated intake forms, digital records, scheduling
- **Vet freedom:** Set own fees, choose work hours (flexible model)
- **Growth:** Access to users outside their geographic area

## Business Model

### Revenue Streams

**1. Consultation Fees (Primary)**
- $35-50 per consultation (market rate)
- **Split:** 30% to Pawprint / 70% to vet
- Example: $40 consult → Pawprint: $12, Vet: $28
- **Target:** 8-12 consultations per vet per week (part-time arrangement)

**2. Pharmacy Commission (Secondary)**
- 12-20% margin on prescription fulfillment (Chewy benchmark: ~30% gross margin)
- Integrated partnerships with Chewy Pharmacy, PetMeds, 1-800-PetMeds
- Expected to grow as consultation volume → prescription volume increases

**3. Vet Subscription Tier (Premium, Optional)**
- $200-300/month for advanced practice management
- Features: Patient analytics, appointment forecasting, performance dashboards
- Target: Top-performing vet partners

### Unit Economics (Target)

| Metric | Target |
|--------|--------|
| **CAC (Pet owner)** | $25-35 (blended: organic SEO + paid social) |
| **LTV (Pet owner)** | $180 (4.5 consultations/year @ $40 avg, 35% retention) |
| **Payback period** | 3-4 months |
| **Blended gross margin** | 32-38% (consultation + pharmacy) |

## Competitive Landscape

| Competitor | Model | Strength | Weakness | Pawprint's Advantage |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Vetster** | Vet-first, independent | Strong vet adoption, flexible pricing | User acquisition cost, discovery friction | Pet owner-first, AI triage, lower cost |
| **Dutch** | Subscription ($19/mo) | Unlimited access, predictable LTV | Higher churn risk, lower per-consult revenue | Per-consult pricing, higher vet revenue share |
| **Chewy Connect** | E-commerce bundled | Massive user base (50M+), convenience | Generic experience, limited vet network | Specialized, better UX for symptoms |
| **AirVet** | Instant triage | Fastest response, mobile-first | Lower brand awareness, smaller network | Complementary (not competitive) |
| **Local vets + Zoom** | Ad-hoc DIY | Relationship-based | Inconsistent, unregulated, no scale | Professionalized, regulated, scalable |

**Market Position:** Pet owner-first platform with AI-guided experience (not just vet marketplace). $30-50 pricing is competitive. 70% vet payout is best-in-class.
